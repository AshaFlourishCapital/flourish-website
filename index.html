Our Story

From scaling startups to summiting peaks: Why we had to build Flourish ourselves.

If you look for standard climate VCs, you usually find a specific archetype. That's not us. We are women who have spent decades inside the machine — scaling massive startups, handling corporate strategy of billion dollar companies, and deploying venture capital into high-impact climate technologies.

But we are also endurance athletes who run and ski the alpine ridges we are trying to save from low snow packs, and daughters who love to sit in a thriving garden with our mums. We believe that a genuine connection with the natural world makes us all more resilient and more clear-eyed about what's actually at stake. Despite years of traditional climate investing, we are witnessing the rapid degradation of nature in real-time — which is why we urgently need new, flexible capital tools deployed right now to fund immediate adaptation and resilience.

We are not separate from the ecosystems we are trying to save. We seek to be in right relationship with them — symbiotic, not extractive. Nature has been quietly absorbing our costs for centuries. We don't take that for granted.

We know how to structure a term sheet, and we know what healthy soil feels like between our toes. You need both to save our wild spaces, our food systems, and the broader ecosystems we need to flourish — from your favorite trail to your backyard garden.

Asha & Charlotte — Marin Headlands

The Team

The right stewards for your capital and the planet.

Asha Agrawal

Co-Founder

Asha brings two decades of experience across venture capital, corporate strategy, and law — deployed at the intersection of climate, impact, and innovation. As sole Managing Director of Tin Shed Ventures, Patagonia's venture capital fund, she led equity and debt investments in early-stage climate startups and scaled Patagonia's Worn Wear resale business to first-in-market profitability, in her concurrent role as Head of Corporate Development.

Prior to Patagonia, Asha moved capital across the capital stack — from hedge funds at Sidley Austin, to venture and startups at Gunderson Dettmer, and then leading investor relations and closing raises of $100M+ and transactions exceeding $500M+ within her executive roles at climate startups. She holds a JD cum laude from the University of Michigan Law School and a BS from Northwestern University.

Off the clock, Asha spends most of her time in nature — trail running in the Marin Headlands (North Face Endurance Trail Marathon), Tahoe (Broken Arrow Sky Race), and the Alps (Tour du Mont Blanc). She spends her winters in the mountains, and lived through the lowest snowpack in recorded Colorado history this past winter — an experience that only deepened her conviction to finding solutions for us all to flourish in a changing landscape.

Charlotte Kirk

Co-Founder

Charlotte brings a decade of expertise spanning technology investments, developments, and operations at the intersection of clean energy innovation and industrial transformation. She led European investments for Clean Energy Ventures across $500M in AUM, deploying capital into high-impact, early-stage climate technologies. Previously, as Head of Technology in the CEO's Office at Fortescue, she led major capital investments and technology programs to enable deep decarbonization, whilst establishing their $100M CVC fund for renewable energy solutions.

She holds a PhD in Chemistry and First Class MChem from the University of Oxford, where she was an academic scholar. She also served for five years in the British Army and was the first woman to pass selection into the infantry. A North Face–sponsored endurance athlete, she has competed at World Championships across ultramarathons, Ironman triathlons, and cross-country skiing, and in 2025 completed 90 consecutive ultramarathons along the 4,500km Pacific Crest Trail.

Experiencing climate impacts firsthand on these adventures has strengthened her drive to deploy the technologies she has spent years developing to protect these outdoor places and communities.
